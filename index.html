Bushfires in Australia

This page provides an overview of bushfires that have occurred in Australia, and the impact of climate change on these occurrences.

Impact of Bushfires on Australian Land Area

This map illustrates the amount of land that was burnt in each state and territory during the 2020-21 bushfire season. This has been normalised using the area of each state to provide a percentage of the land burnt.

Based on the visualisation, the most affected state or territory was the Australian Capital Territory where over a third of the territory's land area was burnt during the bushfire season. The scale of the damage to the land was substantially more detrimental compared to the other states and territories where the next most affected states were New South Wales and Victoria, which only sustained approximately 6% of damage of the state's land.

In contrast, Western Australia, South Australia and Tasmania had less than 1% of their land area that was affected by the bushfires in the 2020-21 bushfire season.

Changes in Climate over the years in Australia

Climate change has had a strong impact on many aspects of bushfires such as their frequency and intensity. For instance, the increased average temperature of the globe, in tandem with decrease rainfall have acted to exacerbate the dry seasons experienced by areas that have historically been suspectible to bushfires.

Below, the key climate factors that have been explored include temperature and rainfall.

Impact of Climate Change on Temperature

The two line charts represents the monthly average maximum and minimum temperature of each state and territory in Australia between the period of 2000-2023.

Overall, between the period of 2000-2023, the mean maximum temperature has remained rather consistent, however, in recent years, there have been more instances of extreme heat such as in the case of 2019 and 2022 where there were instances of the mean maximum temperatures of a state or territory exceeding 35 degrees celsius. A similar trend can be observed in the mean minimum temperature. However, it is worth noting that the mean minimum temperature has a higher variance between each state unlike the maximum temperature.

Impact of Climate Change on Rainfall

The heatmap below depicts the mean monthly rainfall experienced by each individual state and territory, as well as nationally between 2010-2023.

As seen in the heatmap, with the exception of February 2022 which was an outlier, overall the average rainfall has decreased in most states, particularly in the last 5 years.
